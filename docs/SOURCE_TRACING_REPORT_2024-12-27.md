### 4. **Greyson found no significant difference in pre/post-1975 NDEs**

**Status**: ✅ FULLY VERIFIED

```
[E] Greyson, Bruce. (2007). Do Prevailing Societal Models Influence Reports

```

**Key Finding**: Greyson tested the "cultural priming hypothesis" by comparing NDEs reported before and after Raymond Moody's 1975 book popularized the NDE model. **Verification**: This is Greyson's 2007 study specifically designed to test cultural expectancy effects.

**Key Finding**: Feuerverger's Bayesian statistical analysis of the Talpiot Tomb ossuary name cluster yielded 600:1 odds favoring identification as Jesus's family tomb. However, the methodology is **DISPUTED** by other statisticians.

**Caveats**:

- Critics argue odds calculation is inflated due to methodological choices
- Not widely accepted in mainstream New Testament scholarship
- Alternative statistical analyses yield different results

**Recommendation**: Framework should present this as a contested claim with significant scholarly pushback, not established fact.

**Key Finding**: Bauer's landmark thesis demonstrated through geographical and textual forensics that in many regions (Egypt, Edessa, Asia Minor), forms of Christianity later labeled "heretical" (Gnostic, Marcionite) were actually the **original and dominant** manifestations of the faith. **Verification**: This is a foundational work in historical-critical scholarship of early Christianity. While influential and widely cited, it remains contested—not universally accepted but no longer fringe. The framework document accurately represents its significance and scholarly reception.
