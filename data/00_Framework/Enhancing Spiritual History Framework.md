

# **A Coherent Framework for Spiritual History: Weaving the Divine Bricolage**

## **Introduction: The Epistemological Crisis of a Dispersed Archive**

In the contemporary intellectual landscape, the seeker of integrated truth faces a profound epistemological fragmentation. The archive of human experience is vast, yet it is dispersed across domains that frequently operate in mutual isolation or open antagonism. We possess the ancient, mythic narratives of the Near East, which speak of gods, floods, and cosmic battles. We have the intricate, systematic theological architecture of the Enlightenment, most notably the works of Emanuel Swedenborg, which posits a coherent spiritual structure behind the visible world. We have the rigorous, empirical findings of secular archaeology and biblical criticism, which deconstruct these sacred histories into political and literary artifacts. Finally, we are confronted with the burgeoning field of consciousness studies, which presents anomalous data—from Near-Death Experiences (NDEs) to verified past-life memories—that challenges the foundational axioms of materialist science.

To view these streams of data in isolation is to see only chaos: a history of contradictory myths, a theology at odds with science, and a set of empirical anomalies that fit no paradigm. However, this report posits that this fragmentation is artificial, a result of viewing the data solely through a horizontal lens. By integrating the disparate research documents from the Swedenborgian, Biblical, and Consciousness clusters into a single, coherent framework, a continuous spiritual history of humanity is revealed. This is not a history of random events, but a trajectory guided by the adaptive influx of the Divine.1

The central thesis of this report is that human history is the story of how the Divine has continuously adapted its communication to the evolving cognitive and spiritual state of humanity. As the human mind shifted from internal perception to external language, and from tribalism to empire, the "Divine Influx" adapted its vessel—moving from the internal respiration of the Paleolithic to the allegorical myths of the Bronze Age, to the historical texts of the Iron Age, and finally to the Incarnation of the Word itself.

The foundational hermeneutic for this synthesis is the Swedenborgian "Doctrine of Correspondences." This universal law states that the natural world (the plane of effects) corresponds directly to the spiritual world (the plane of causes). Every object, event, and process in the natural realm is the necessary external "body" or "ultimate" that serves as a vessel for an internal, spiritual "soul".1 This report establishes a new, two-tiered epistemology that unifies faith and reason by accepting the findings of secular science as the accurate map of the "body," while applying spiritual principles to reveal the "soul."

## **Part I: The Primordial Mind and the Cognitive Archaeology of the "Golden Age"**

To reconstruct a coherent spiritual history, one must first identify the baseline from which humanity departed. Theological traditions speak of a "Golden Age" or "Most Ancient Church," a time of direct communication with the Divine. Modern secular inquiry speaks of the Lower Paleolithic and the slow evolution of hominin cognition. A rigorous synthesis reveals that these are not contradictory accounts but different descriptions of the same reality. The "Golden Age" is not a fable; it is an allegorical memory of the specific cognitive architecture of early humanity.1

### **1.1. The Physiology of Internal Influx: Respiration and the Vagus Nerve**

Emanuel Swedenborg’s theological description of the "Most Ancient Church" centers on a specific physiological mechanism: "internal respiration." He describes a state where the internal mind and the natural mind acted in perfect harmony, facilitated by a "silent, profound mode of thought" that was inextricably linked to the rhythm of the breath.1 This state allowed for a direct perception of spiritual truths, unmediated by external language.

This theological insight finds a precise correlate in paleoanthropology. The evolution of bipedalism in early hominins required a fundamental anatomical shift: the decoupling of breathing from locomotion. Unlike quadrupeds, whose breathing is forced by the stride, bipedal hominins gained voluntary control over their respiratory cycles. This adaptation was the biological prerequisite for the evolution of complex cognition and, eventually, speech.1

Furthermore, 21st-century neuroscience provides the functional explanation for why this "internal respiration" would correspond to a "Golden Age" of perception. Research into "neurotheology" and the mechanics of meditation confirms that controlled, slow, non-vocal breathing stimulates the vagus nerve. This stimulation triggers the parasympathetic nervous system, inducing a state of autonomic relaxation and "functional deafferentation" of the parietal lobe—the brain region responsible for distinguishing the "self" from the "world".1 Thus, the "internal respiration" described by Swedenborg was a biological reality that facilitated a state of "self-transcendence" and high cognitive receptivity, allowing early hominins to exist in a continuous state of deep, non-verbal connection with their environment.

### **1.2. Gestural Communication and the "Lip-Speech" Paradox**

Theologically, the "Most Ancient" people are described as having no articulate words. Instead, they communicated through "innumerable changes of the... lips" and facial muscles, a "lip-speech" that was far more expressive and immediate than spoken language.1

Scientifically, this aligns with the "Gestural Theory" of language origins. As the hands of early hominins were liberated for tool use (Acheulean technology), the facial musculature was simultaneously freed from the rigid demands of tearing raw meat. This evolutionary relaxation allowed the face to be "co-opted" for complex visual communication.1 Before the larynx descended to permit articulate speech—a process spanning hundreds of thousands of years—humanity likely possessed a highly sophisticated mimetic and gestural language. This "lip-speech" was not a primitive precursor to language but a distinct mode of communication that relied on immediate visual empathy rather than abstract auditory symbols, mirroring the theological description of a time when thoughts were shared instantly and silently.3

### **1.3. The Cognitive Science of "Correspondences"**

The central cognitive feature of this primordial state was "Representative Language"—the innate ability to perceive abstract spiritual meaning in concrete natural objects. To the "Golden Age" mind, a mountain was not merely a geological formation but was instantaneously perceived as "heaven" or "the Lord".1

Cognitive science validates this as a fundamental structure of the human mind, known as **Conceptual Metaphor Theory (CMT)**. CMT posits that all abstract human thought is grounded in concrete, sensorimotor experiences. We cannot think about "progress" without the metaphor of "forward motion," nor "importance" without the metaphor of "size".1 Cognitive archaeology places the origin of this capacity in the Lower Paleolithic. The ability to see a "handaxe" inside a raw flint nodule requires the same cognitive leap as seeing "heaven" in a "mountain": the ability to perceive a non-existent ideal form within a physical object.

Moreover, the Cognitive Science of Religion (CSR) identifies a "teleological bias" in the human brain—an innate, early-developing intuition that the natural world is purposefully designed. This is not a learned cultural trait but a "cognitive default" present in young children and persisting in adults unless actively overridden.4 Thus, the "Doctrine of Correspondences" is not merely a theological dogma; it is the theological name for the "teleological bias," the innate cognitive wiring that allows humanity to perceive the "plane of causes" within the "plane of effects."

## **Part II: The Echo of the Ancient Word – Traces of a Lost Knowledge**

Swedenborg posits that following the "Golden Age," but prior to the Hebrew Bible, there existed a universal "Ancient Word." He explicitly classifies the narratives of Genesis 1-11 as "made-up history" derived from this lost source.5 Skeptics might view this as a convenient fiction to explain biblical allegory. However, an interdisciplinary analysis of oral tradition, mnemonics, and prehistoric semiotics provides tangible evidence for this "Ancient Word."

### **2.1. The "Memory Code" and the Durability of Oral Tradition**

The concept of a lost, universal system of knowledge is supported by the research of Lynne Kelly and others into "Primary Orality." Pre-literate cultures were not ignorant; they possessed "Knowledge of Caliber" encoded in the landscape itself. The "Songlines" of the Australian Aboriginals serve as a living model of this.7

* **Geological Memory:** Aboriginal traditions accurately describe coastal geographies and land bridges that were submerged by rising sea levels over 7,000 years ago.7  
* **Astronomical Memory:** Palawa traditions preserve an account of the star Canopus appearing near the southern celestial pole—a position it has not held for approximately 14,000 years.7  
* **The Klamath Echo:** In North America, the Klamath people preserve a precise geological account of the eruption of Mount Mazama (Crater Lake), which occurred 7,700 years ago. The oral tradition accurately details the "red-hot rocks," the collapse of the summit, and the formation of the caldera.7

These examples demonstrate that oral cultures utilized a "Cognitive Technology"—associating information with physical locations—to preserve data for millennia. The "Ancient Word" described by Swedenborg was likely this very system: a universal method of encoding spiritual and practical truth into the landscape, making the world itself a sacred text.7

### **2.2. The Mathematical Fingerprint: A Structural Analysis**

If the "Ancient Word" was a cognitive system, does it leave a structural fingerprint? A comparative analysis of Swedenborg’s theological numerology and the numerical systems of the Paleolithic reveals a striking "Cognitive Echo".8

Swedenborg’s system is constructed exclusively from the prime numbers **2, 3, 5, and 7**.

* **2** corresponds to Union and Good.  
* **3** corresponds to Fullness and Truth.  
* **5** corresponds to Quantity and Sufficiency.  
* **7** corresponds to Holiness.8

Remarkably, these same primes form the structural basis of prehistoric mnemonic systems:

* **The Paleolithic Lexicon:** Genevieve von Petzinger has identified a stable lexicon of exactly **32** geometric signs used across Europe for 30,000 years. The number 32 is $2^5$, a construct of the primes 2 and 5\.8  
* **Stonehenge:** The Sarsen Circle consists of **30** uprights ($2 \\times 3 \\times 5$). The Aubrey Holes number **56** ($2^3 \\times 7$). The Trilithons number **5**.8

This convergence does not imply a direct historical lineage from the Ice Age to 18th-century Sweden. Rather, it suggests that both the prehistoric mind and Swedenborg’s systematic theology tapped into the same "cognitive affordance" of small prime numbers to organize reality. The "Ancient Word" was a system that utilized the innate mathematical properties of the human mind to structure the universe of meaning.8

### **2.3. The Shift to External Memory and the "Fall"**

The "Fall," in this framework, describes the "Great Decoupling." It was the transition from the internal, landscape-based memory of the "Ancient Word" (Songlines) to the external, artificial memory of the agricultural state (Monuments and Text).  
The theological narrative of "Eve" (external sensation) turning away from "Adam" (internal perception) 1 mirrors the cognitive shift required by the Neolithic Revolution. As societies became sedentary, they could no longer rely on the landscape for memory; they had to build artificial memory palaces (megaliths) and eventually develop writing. This externalization severed the direct link to the spiritual "plane of causes," necessitating a new form of revelation: the written Myth.7

## **Part III: The Forging of the Narrative – Mythic Bricolage and the Evolution of Consciousness**

With the loss of internal perception, the Divine Influx required a new vessel: the Mythic Narrative. However, the form these myths took was not arbitrary. It was shaped by the "Consciousness-Driven Evolution" (CDE) of humanity.

### **3.1. Consciousness as a Selective Pressure: The CDE Hypothesis**

The CDE hypothesis posits that consciousness is not an epiphenomenon but a fundamental force in memetic evolution. The "ruling love" of a society—its collective orientation toward either "Love of Neighbor" (Trajectory A) or "Love of Self/Power" (Trajectory B)—acts as a selective pressure, shaping which myths survive and how they are adapted.9

This process creates a "Memetic Niche." Just as beavers construct dams to alter their physical environment, human consciousness constructs an ideological niche.

* **Trajectory B (Fragmentation):** A society dominated by the *proprium* (self-love) constructs a niche that selects for myths of conflict and hierarchy. This leads to "Social Fragmentation," characterized by inequality and coercive power.11  
* **Trajectory A (Coherence):** A society guided by Divine Influx constructs a niche that selects for myths of unity and dignity. This leads to "Social Cohesion," characterized by trust and shared purpose.11

### **3.2. Genesis as Theological Bricolage**

The application of this hypothesis explains the divergence of the biblical narrative from its Ancient Near Eastern (ANE) context. Both Israel and Babylon inherited a common "proto-myth" of creation involving primordial waters.

* **Babylon (Trajectory B):** In the *Enuma Elish*, the collective consciousness of an imperial state adapted the myth into a justification for hierarchy. The primordial water becomes *Tiamat*, a monster to be slain. Creation is an act of *Theomachy* (god-war), and humanity is created as slaves to the gods. This myth stabilizes a stratified, coercive society.3  
* **Israel (Trajectory A):** The authors of Genesis 1, operating under the influence of a "Trajectory A" consciousness, engaged in "Mythic Bricolage" to *purify* the narrative. They retained the primordial water (*tehom*) but stripped it of personality. They replaced combat with Divine Fiat ("And God said"). Most radically, they elevated humanity from slave status to the *Imago Dei* (Image of God). This was not a new invention but a "restorative adaptation"—a conscious rewriting of the cultural script to reflect a higher spiritual reality.3

### **3.3. The Paradox of the Exodus: "Real History" vs. "Mythic History"**

Moving from the allegorical primeval history (Genesis 1-11) to the patriarchal and Exodus narratives (Genesis 12 onward), Swedenborg asserts a shift to "Real History".5 Yet, secular scholarship finds no archaeological evidence for the mass Exodus described in the text.

The solution lies in understanding the Exodus as a **"Providentially Guided Composite."** The biblical text is a "Charter Myth" forged from disparate but authentic "memory fragments" of the Late Bronze Age (LBA).

1. **The Memory of Cataclysm:** The narrative of the plagues preserves the memory of the Thera (Santorini) eruption (c. 1600 BCE), which devastated the Mediterranean.1  
2. **The Memory of Deliverance:** The "parting of the sea" preserves the memory of a recurring natural phenomenon, a "wind setdown," in the Nile Delta.1  
3. **The Memory of Liberation:** The core theme of slavery is grounded in the authentic experience of Canaanite populations under Egyptian imperial rule. The "Merneptah Stele" (1207 BCE) confirms the existence of a people named "Israel" in Canaan, likely a coalition of indigenous groups and escaped slaves (the *'Apiru* or *Shasu*) who threw off the Egyptian yoke during the LBA Collapse.3

The Divine Influx acted as the "gravitational core," drawing these historically separate elements into a single narrative orbit. The resulting story is "true" not because it is a videotape of a single event, but because it perfectly corresponds to the spiritual process of regeneration.

* **Egypt** corresponds to the Natural Mind.  
* **Pharaoh** corresponds to the *Proprium* (the false self).  
* **The Exodus** corresponds to the liberation of the Soul.

Thus, the scribes were "bricoleurs" of the Divine, assembling the "body" of history from the debris of the past to create a perfect vessel for the "soul" of the Word.6

## **Part IV: The Symbolic Architecture of the Human Experience**

Within this "Real History," the Divine utilized specific human roles as the primary symbols of spiritual reality. To read the "soul" of the text, one must decode these correspondences, which map the anatomy of the human psyche and the Divine nature.2

### **4.1. The Fundamental Duality: Male and Female**

Swedenborgian theology posits that the distinction between male and female is not merely biological but spiritual, corresponding to the two primary faculties of the human mind.

* **Male** corresponds to **Intellect/Truth** (the understanding).  
* **Female** corresponds to **Will/Good** (the affection/love).2

This correspondence is rooted in the dynamic of "Conjunction." Truth (Male) provides the form, but Good (Female) provides the life and the motivation. The spiritual goal of humanity is the "Heavenly Marriage"—the perfect union of the Will and the Understanding. In a "celestial" state (ruled by love), the Wife corresponds to Truth and the Husband to Good; in a "spiritual" state (ruled by truth), this reverses.2 This dynamic interplay underscores that spiritual wholeness requires the integration of both "head" and "heart."

### **4.2. The Governance of the Spirit: King and Priest**

The political offices of ancient Israel serve as external models for internal governance.

* **The King** corresponds to **Divine Truth**. The King brings order, judgment, and structure to the kingdom (the mind).  
* **The Priest** corresponds to **Divine Good**. The Priest facilitates worship, sanctification, and connection with the Divine.2

A mind governed only by the "King" (Truth) without the "Priest" (Good) becomes harsh and legalistic. A mind governed only by the "Priest" without the "King" becomes sentimental and formless. The biblical narrative’s history of kings and priests is a dramatization of the struggle to balance these internal forces.2

### **4.3. The Divine Archetypes: Father and Son**

Crucially, this symbolic logic extends to the Godhead. The "Father" and "Son" are not separate persons but aspects of the One Divine Person.

* **The Father** corresponds to **Divine Good** (the Soul/Essence).  
* **The Son** corresponds to **Divine Truth** (the Body/Manifestation).2

Just as the soul is invisible and only known through the body, the infinite Divine Love (Father) is unknowable except through its manifestation in Divine Truth (Son). This "Divine Marriage" is the source of all creation.

The trajectory of history—from the internal perception of the Golden Age to the external laws of the Iron Age—culminated in the Incarnation. This was the moment the Influx *became* the Vessel.

### **5.1. The Glorification: The Reality Behind the "Historical Jesus"**

Secular scholarship searches for the "Historical Jesus" by stripping away the divine attributes. Swedenborgian theology argues that the "Historical Jesus"—the finite, human man born of Mary—was indeed distinct from the Divine, but that his life was a process of **"Glorification"**.1

Glorification was the progressive union of the external Human with the internal Divine. This was achieved through **Temptation Combats**.

* **Baptism** marked the initiation of this combat, the acceptance of the mission to confront the hereditary evil of the human race.1  
* **The Crucifixion** was not a tragic defeat or a penal substitution; it was the final, supreme temptation. By enduring the cross, the Lord fully expelled the finite, maternal human and fully united with the Divine Father.1

This framework reinterprets the "Criterion of Embarrassment" used by biblical scholars. The moments where Jesus appears weak, ignorant, or in despair are not evidence against his divinity; they are the authentic "footprints" of the Temptation process—the very mechanism of his victory.1

* **Evidence:** Modern Near-Death Experience (NDE) research provides the strongest data. Experiencers consistently encounter a "Being of Light" who radiates **Unconditional Love** and **Non-Judgment**.  
* **Identification:** Crucially, experiencers from diverse backgrounds spontaneously identify this Being as **Jesus Christ**. This is not a cultural projection but a "pure identification" based on the personality match with the Divine Human of the Gospels.12

#### **The Path of Gnosis (The Path of Dismantling)**

This path represents the resistance of the *proprium* (self-love). Its defining strategy is to *dismantle* the specific identity of the Divine Human to "establish ourselves as the prime suspects for gnosis".12 It employs three primary tactics:

## **Part VII: The Threefold Path of the Soul – A Unified Afterlife Model**

What happens after the biological machine stops? The "Dispersed Archive" offers conflicting answers: the Christian Heaven, the Eastern Reincarnation, or the secular Oblivion. A synthesis of Swedenborg, NDEs, and the Division of Perceptual Studies (DOPS) reveals a "Multi-Axial" reality.14

### **7.1. The Evidence for Survival: A Challenge to Materialism**

Materialist science dismisses NDEs as the hallucinations of a "dying brain." However, the data presents insuperable challenges to this view:

* **Veridical Perception:** In NDEs, patients often report details (e.g., a shoe on a ledge, conversation in a waiting room) that were physically impossible for them to perceive.  
* **The Challenge of Lucidity:** Brain hypoxia causes confusion and fragmentation. NDEs are characterized by "hyper-lucidity" and structure ("more real than real"), which is the neurological opposite of a dying brain.11

### **7.2. Path One: The Normative Path (Linear)**

For the majority of souls, the trajectory is linear, matching the Swedenborgian and standard NDE model.

* **The Passage:** The soul separates, moves through a "tunnel" (transition), and is greeted by a "Welcoming Committee" (angels/relatives).1  
* **The Life Review:** This is the central mechanism of judgment. It is not external but internal. The soul relives its life pan-optically, feeling the effects of its actions on others. This reveals the "Ruling Love" undeniable. The soul then gravitates to the community (Heaven or Hell) that shares this love.1

### **7.3. Path Two: The Restorative Incarnation (Cyclical Exception)**

The DOPS data on children with past-life memories reveals a critical anomaly: 70% of these children recall a violent or unnatural death.11  
This suggests that reincarnation is not the standard "wheel of karma" but a "Restorative Mechanism."

* **The Theory:** A life cut short by trauma leaves the "seed-state" of the ruling love incomplete. The system of consciousness is compassionate; it allows a "do-over" or "restorative incarnation" to allow the soul to complete its identity formation before proceeding to the spiritual world.10

### **7.4. Path Three: The Volunteer Soul (Service)**

The CDE hypothesis and NDE data suggest a third path for advanced souls: the **Volunteer**.

* **The Theory:** Souls that have already achieved coherence (Trajectory A) may choose to incarnate to inject "high-coherence memes" (e.g., universal love) into a "Trajectory B" culture. These individuals often exhibit a precocious sense of mission and a resistance to cultural conditioning.10
