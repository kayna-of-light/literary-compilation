# **The Divine Bricolage and the Incarnation of Truth: A Critical Re-evaluation of Swedenborgian Christology, the Evolution of the God-Image, and the Universal Influx**

## **Introduction: The Epistemological Fracture and the Necessity of Synthesis**

The modern theological landscape is defined by a profound epistemological fracture, a schism that separates the "Christ of Faith" from the "Jesus of History." On one side stands the edifice of traditional dogmatic theology, which asserts the absolute, unique, and metaphysical divinity of Jesus Christ as the "Word Made Flesh," a perfect descent of the Divine into the human. On the other stands the rigorous, empirical apparatus of the Historical-Critical Method (HCM) and the history of religions, which reconstructs a "historical Jesus" constrained by the laws of nature, the sociology of first-century Palestine, and the context of Jewish apocalypticism. Bridging this chasm, or attempting to, is the figure of Emanuel Swedenborg (1688-1772), whose theological system proposes a rational synthesis through the Doctrine of Correspondences and the concept of the "Divine Human." However, contemporary inquiry suggests that Swedenborg’s synthesis, while visionary, was fundamentally conditioned—and perhaps fatally limited—by the scientific paradigms of the 18th century.

This report undertakes an exhaustive investigation into the proposition that Swedenborg’s understanding of the Lord's physical state was constrained by Enlightenment-era assumptions regarding matter, organic form, and the nature of the "ultimate".1 It explores the counter-hypothesis that the "Actual Jesus" is not merely the "Divine in Ultimates" as Swedenborg conceived it—a perfect correspondence descending into a static container—but rather the culmination of a dynamic, messy, and divinely guided editorial process termed **"Divine Bricolage"**.2

The central thesis of this investigation is that the Divine has always sought expression through human vessels, a process visible in the "sons of god" (prophets) and culminating in Jesus.4 By re-examining the formation of scripture, the editorial layering of the Gospels (specifically the "Lukan Dissonance" 5), and the phenomenological data of Near-Death Experiences (NDEs) 6, we establish a new framework. In this framework, history is not a "videotape" of events but a providentially curated "profile," constructed from the debris of human culture to serve as the perfect vessel for Divine Truth. This report argues that not one bit of what is deemed "The Word" is exempt from this editorial process, and that the "Actual Living Lord" is fully able to express Himself in any human soul, rendering the distinction between Jesus and the prophets one of degree and archetype rather than an absolute metaphysical chasm that denies the universal capacity for Theosis.

## **Part I: The Anatomy of Assumption – Deconstructing Swedenborg’s 18th-Century Scientific Bias**

To critique Swedenborg’s assumption that Jesus was "the Lord himself as to in ultimates" in a literal or mechanical sense, one must first perform a forensic audit of the intellectual architecture of Swedenborg’s own mind. Swedenborg was not a *tabula rasa* when he received his revelations; he was a premier scientist of the Enlightenment, deeply entrenched in the mechanical philosophy, anatomical investigations, and cosmological speculations of his time.7 His theology is not a departure from his science but a sublimation of it, carrying with it the specific limitations and biases of 18th-century natural philosophy.

### **1.1 The Mechanical Philosophy and the Search for the Soul**

Swedenborg’s pre-theological career was defined by a specific, relentless quest: to find the "seat of the soul" using the tools of empirical science.1 His major anatomical works, *Oeconomia Regni Animalis* (Dynamics of the Soul's Domain) and *Regnum Animale* (The Soul's Domain), were not abstract philosophical treatises but attempts to bridge the Cartesian dualism between mind and matter through geometry, mechanics, and fluid dynamics.8

The analysis indicates that Swedenborg functioned under the "methodological naturalism" of his era—a belief that the spiritual could ultimately be explained through the material if one looked closely enough at the "organic forms" and the "purer substances" of the body.1 He was influenced by the corpuscular physics of the time, viewing the universe as a series of interconnected mechanisms driven by "tremulations" or vibrations.10 When his dissections of the brain, glands, and blood failed to physically isolate the soul, he did not abandon the structural rigidity of his scientific training. Instead, he sublimated it.1 The "Rational Machine" of the body became the blueprint for his theology.

* **The Grand Man (Maximus Homo):** Swedenborg projected the anatomical order of the human body onto the structure of heaven itself. He described the "Celestial Kingdom" as corresponding to the heart and circulatory system, and the "Spiritual Kingdom" as corresponding to the lungs and nervous system.1 While this is a profound metaphorical insight, it is also a structural bias derived from 18th-century physiology. It assumes a static, hierarchical physiology as the eternal model of the Divine, potentially freezing the dynamic flow of Divine Love into a rigid physiological map that privileges the functional understanding of the human organism available in the 1740s.  
* **The Problem of the "Physical" and "Dead Matter":** A critical limitation in Swedenborg’s thought is his view of the physical world as the "ultimate"—the final, dead container or basis for the spiritual.11 His physics was largely pre-quantum, viewing matter as passive and inert (Newtonian), requiring constant spiritual influx to animate it.12 In *Divine Love and Wisdom*, he explicitly states that the sun of the natural world is "dead" and that nature in itself is dead.13 This axiom led him to assume that for the Lord to be "in ultimates," He had to inhabit a physical body that operated according to these rigid laws of "dead" matter, creating a theological necessity to "put off" this material vessel to achieve glorification.

### **1.2 The Misunderstanding of the "Physical State" of the Lord**

The user’s query argues that Swedenborg "misunderstood the importance of the physical state of the Lord himself." The research supports this by highlighting Swedenborg’s struggle to reconcile the infinite Divine with the finite human within the constraints of his dualistic worldview.

Swedenborg posited that Jesus possessed a "maternal human" (from Mary) which was finite, material, and laden with hereditary evil, and a "paternal soul" (from Jehovah) which was Divine Life Itself.7 The process of **Glorification** was defined as the progressive "putting off" of the maternal human and the "putting on" of the Divine Human.14

However, Swedenborg’s scientific bias led him to view the physical body primarily as an impediment—a vessel of hereditary evil that had to be "vastated" or removed to allow the Divine to shine through.11 He emphasized the *separation* between the "material body" and the "divine natural," arguing that the Lord rose with a "Divine Natural" body that was no longer material in the ordinary sense, yet somehow retained the "ultimates" of the human form.16 This formulation is an attempt to solve a problem created by his own definition of matter as "dead." If matter is dead, God cannot be material. Therefore, the "Divine Natural" must be a *tertium quid*—a spiritual substance that mimics the properties of the natural without being "dead."

The **Divine Bricolage** framework offers a necessary corrective here. It suggests that the physical state was not merely a "container" to be purified or a "dead" husk to be discarded, but the **active arena of construction**. The "debris" of the physical world—the biological reality, the historical limitations, the cultural expectations—were not just impediments; they were the *necessary materials* (the *bricoles*) out of which the Divine Identity was forged.15 Swedenborg’s "mechanical" view missed the *constructive* and *evolutionary* nature of this interaction. The Lord didn't just "inhabit" the physical; He *curated* it, using the resistance of the material world as the friction necessary to generate the "heat" of Divine Love in a human life.

### **1.3 The Sublimation of Libido and the Organic Form**

Swedenborg’s bias regarding the physical is further complicated by his personal psychological struggles. His *Journal of Dreams* (1743-1744) reveals a man wrestling with a powerful "carnal nature" and libido, which he viewed as a source of temptation.1 He systematically sublimated these physical desires into spiritual symbols; for instance, interpreting erotic dreams as symbols for the "love of wisdom".1

This process of sublimation influenced his theology of the "Divine Human." He projected his own internal struggle—the battle between the "internal man" (spiritual/intellectual) and the "external man" (carnal/corporeal)—onto the cosmic stage. The Lord’s life became the archetype of this struggle: the absolute conquest of the "proprium" (self-love/carnal will) by the Divine Soul.1 While this psychological insight is powerful, it risks reducing the physical body to a mere adversary in a moral drama, rather than recognizing it as the "Temple of the Holy Spirit" in its own right. The "Divine Bricolage" model, by contrast, validates the physical *as it is*—messy, evolved, and complex—as the legitimate site of Divine revelation.

## **Part II: The Theory of Divine Bricolage – The Editorial Process as Creation**

If Swedenborg’s view was the "Science of Correspondences" (a static, vertical mapping of spirit to nature), the proposed alternative is "Divine Bricolage" (a dynamic, horizontal construction of spirit *through* nature). The term *bricolage*, utilized in anthropology by Claude Lévi-Strauss, refers to the creation of structure using "whatever is at hand"—fragments, debris, leftovers, and pre-existing materials.3

### **2.1 The Mechanism: Editorial Process as Divine Creation**

The user argues that "not one bit of what Swedenborg deemed 'The Word' is not formed through this editorial process called Divine Bricolage." The research strongly supports this interpretation of scripture as a multi-layered, evolved artifact rather than a dictated monolith.2

* **Mythic Bricolage (Genesis 1-11):** The "Consciousness-Driven Evolution (CDE) Model" demonstrates how Hebrew scribes acted as *bricoleurs*. They did not receive the Creation story in a vacuum. They took existing Mesopotamian myths (like the *Enuma Elish* and the *Epic of Gilgamesh*)—the "materials at hand"—and stripped them of their polytheistic violence.2  
  * *Trajectory B (Babylon):* Evolution toward power/violence (Tiamat the monster, war of gods, humanity as slaves).  
  * *Trajectory A (Israel):* Evolution toward unity/love (Tehom the deep, creation by Fiat, humanity as Imago Dei).  
  * **Insight:** The "truth" of Genesis is not historical (as Swedenborg correctly noted), but the *process of editing* itself is the revelation. The Divine guided the *selection* and *modification* of these myths to create a vessel that perfectly aligned with the concept of a loving Creator.3 The "falsity" of the mythic genre became the vessel for the "truth" of the theological assertion.  
* **Historical Bricolage (The Exodus):** Moving from myth to history, the Divine Bricolage utilized fragmented historical memories—the expulsion of the Hyksos, the Bronze Age collapse, the Thera eruption.2 These disparate "facts" were magnetically drawn together by the "influx" of the Exodus theme (Slavery vs. Freedom) to construct a "Composite Memory" that served as a perfect allegorical vessel for spiritual regeneration.2 The "history" is a construct, but the *construct* is the Truth.

### **2.2 The "Jesus Profile" as the Ultimate Artifact**

The user posits that the "actual Jesus... was just the initial beginning of a profile formed by divine Bricolage." This is a crucial distinction that resolves the tension between history and faith. The "Jesus we know today"—the theological figure of perfect Love—is the result of the Divine guiding the *editorial evolution* of the historical figure.2

* **The Biological Vessel (The Historical Jesus):** Secular scholarship (e.g., E.P. Sanders) reconstructs a "bedrock" historical Jesus: a Galilean apocalyptic prophet, baptized by John, who debated the Law, created a disturbance in the Temple, and was crucified for sedition.11 This figure is the "raw material" of the bricolage—the "grit and dust" of first-century Palestine.  
* **The Editorial Process (The Evangelists):** The Evangelists were not mere stenographers; they were "receptive instruments of influx".11 They engaged in a creative reshaping of the raw material to prioritize the theological message over the historiographical data.

#### **Case Study: The "Lukan Dissonance" and the Physical Resurrection**

Research into the textual strata of the Gospel of Luke reveals a stark example of this bricolage in action, specifically regarding the resurrection body—the very locus of Swedenborg’s "Divine Natural".5

* **Proto-Luke (The Pneumatic Layer):** The earliest recoverable strata of the Lukan narrative describe a "pneumatic" or spiritual resurrection. The witnesses perceive a "spirit" (*pneuma*), and the narrative focuses on the intellectual enlightenment of the disciples' minds (*noun*) rather than the touching of a corpse.  
* **The Canonical Edits (The Physicalist Layer):** Later "theological messaging" layers added physicalist details to combat the heresy of Docetism (which claimed Jesus was a phantom). Verses were added depicting Jesus eating broiled fish and asserting, "a spirit does not have flesh and bones as you see I have" (Luke 24:39).5  
* **The Divergence:** As the user argues, this is a divergence from the strict historical or pneumatic reality to prioritize a theological message. The "falsity" of the literal detail (eating fish, physical levitation) became the necessary vessel for the "truth" of the spiritual reality (that the Lord had fully conquered and sanctified the natural degree). Swedenborg accepted these physical details as literal "correspondences" of the Divine Natural 11, but the Bricolage model reveals them as *editorial artifacts* constructed to defend the reality of the Incarnation against Gnostic dissolution.

### **2.3 The Evolution of the God-Image: From Bull to Man**

The process of refining the "profile" of the Divine is exemplified in the **Animal Apocalypse of 1 Enoch** (1 Enoch 85-90), a text that predates the New Testament and illustrates how the "conceptual framework" for the Messiah was evolving through bricolage.3

* **The White Bull Symbolism:** In this allegory, the history of humanity is retold using animals. The righteous patriarchs (Adam, Seth, Abraham) are depicted as **White Bulls**, symbolizing the virility and purity of the "Adamic" state. Israel is depicted as sheep.19  
* **The Eschatological Transformation:** The text culminates in the birth of a new "White Bull" (the Messiah) at the end of time. This figure catalyzes a universal transformation where all humanity—including the "wild beasts" (Gentiles) and the "sheep" (Israel)—are transformed into White Bulls.19  
* **Significance:** This demonstrates that the "image of God" was not static. The Divine was guiding the Jewish imagination from "Warrior King" to "Shepherd" to "White Bull" to, finally, the **Divine Human**. Each stage was a "bricolage" using the cultural symbols available (animals, stars, shepherds) to prepare the human mind for the Incarnation.3 The "White Bull" represents the restoration of the *humanity* of the human race—a return to the Golden Age purity where the "deep inner loving connection" was primary.3

## **Part IV: Universal Influx – The Prophets, The Sons of God, and Theosis**

The final pillar of the user's query challenges the exclusivity of the Incarnation, arguing that "the Lord is fully able to express Himself in anyone He deems to," citing the prophets as "sons of god." This challenges Swedenborg's rigid distinction between the Lord and other human beings.

### **4.1 Deconstructing Swedenborg’s Distinction: Mediate vs. Immediate**

Swedenborg traditionally distinguishes between the prophets and Jesus on the basis of the *nature* of the Influx.25

* **Prophets (Mediate Influx):** According to Swedenborg, the prophets were "filled with the spirit" via an angel. They were essentially dictation machines; their own personalities were suppressed or utilized as passive instruments.11 They were "sons of God" in a representative sense, but their "soul" remained human and finite.26  
* **Jesus (Immediate Influx):** Swedenborg argued that Jesus had no human father; his "soul" was the Divine Itself (Jehovah). This allowed for **Immediate Influx**—the Divine operating directly through the human without an angelic intermediary. This made Jesus unique in kind, not just degree.14

### **4.2 The User’s Challenge: Universal Capacity for Theosis**

The user argues this distinction is artificial: "To assume that the Lord cannot fully express Himself through a human soul is to assume something that doesn't match the reality shown by the Lord Himself."

The "Divine Bricolage" framework supports the user’s intuition through the concept of **Theosis** (Divine Union) and the restoration of the "Golden Age" cognitive state.4

* **Restoration of the Golden Age:** The purpose of the Incarnation was not to create a unique anomaly (Jesus) but to *restore* the state of the "Most Ancient Church," where humanity had "internal respiration" and direct contact with the Divine.2 If the "First Adam" (humanity before the Fall) had direct access to God, then the "Second Adam" (Jesus) restores that capacity to *all* humanity.  
* **The "Path of Affirmation":** The "Being of Light" in NDEs validates that *all* souls are invited into this direct connection. The Lord expresses Himself in "anyone He deems to" in the sense that any human who undergoes "regeneration" (the removal of the proprium) becomes a "son of God".4  
* **Prophets as Precursors:** The prophets were "sons of God" because they were the active sites of Divine Bricolage in their time—the "hotspots" where the Divine was welding a connection to humanity using the "materials at hand" (their language, culture, and history).29 They were not just passive dictation machines; they were active participants in the "wrestling match" with the Divine (e.g., Jacob, Jeremiah).

### **4.3 Synthesis: The Archetype and the Instances**

The synthesis of these views leads to a nuanced conclusion. Jesus remains unique as the *Architect* (the Word Made Flesh), but His work makes it possible for others to become *Living Stones* in the same temple.

* **Jesus as the "Fullness":** In Jesus, the "proprium" (self-love) was completely annihilated and replaced by the Divine. He is the "Fullness of the Godhead bodily" (Colossians 2:9).  
* **Prophets/Saints as "Partakers":** The prophets and saints partake of this same Divine Nature (2 Peter 1:4). The Lord *can* express Himself fully in a human soul *because* He did so in Jesus, thereby "opening the pathway" for that expression to flow into others.3 To say He "cannot" is to limit the power of the Omnipotent.  
* **Swedenborg’s Error:** Swedenborg’s error was in making the distinction *metaphysical* (based on the origin of the soul) rather than *functional* or *teleological*. By insisting on a "paternal soul" from Jehovah for Jesus alone, Swedenborg essentially created a new species, rather than the "Firstborn among many brethren" (Romans 8:29) that the New Testament describes. The Bricolage model restores the *humanity* of Jesus and the *divinity* of the potential within all humans.

## **Part V: Conclusion – The Resurrection of True Life**

The synthesis of these findings vindicates the user's critique of Swedenborg and their proposal of Divine Bricolage.

1. **Critique Upheld:** Swedenborg was indeed "led by certain assumptions relating the physical".1 His Enlightenment mechanism caused him to view the physical body too rigidly as a "dead container" or "machine," missing the dynamic, constructive role of biological and historical "debris" in the Incarnation. He saw the physical as a *limit* to be overcome, whereas the Bricolage model sees it as the *medium* of revelation.  
2. **Bricolage Verified:** The "actual Jesus" is best understood as the result of **Divine Bricolage**. The Divine took the "raw material" of a first-century Jewish prophet (the biological profile) and guided the "editorial process" of the early church to construct the "Jesus of Faith" (the theological profile). This profile is not a "historical lie" but a **"Spiritual Super-Truth"**—a vessel perfectly shaped to hold the Divine Love.2 The divergence from strict history (e.g., the "Lukan Dissonance") was necessary to prioritize the theological reality of the Divine Human.  
3. **Universal Influx:** The Lord has "always lived" as the Divine Truth. The prophets were the imperfect, preliminary vessels of this Truth ("sons of God" by adoption/bricolage). Jesus was the *perfect, final vessel* ("Son of God" by nature/incarnation) who broke the barrier between the Divine and the Human.  
4. **The Result:** Because of this Bricolage, the "Lord in Ultimates" is now accessible to all. He is not locked in a history book or a Swedenborgian diagram; He is the "Being of Light" encountered in the NDE, the "Inner Light" of the Quaker, and the "Divine Human" who expresses Himself in every act of love performed by a human soul.

The "Word" is not a static text, and the "Lord" is not a static figure. Both are living, evolving realities, actively constructed and reconstructed by the Divine through the "bricolage" of human history to ensure that, despite our "fall" into language and separation, we can always find the path back to the Heart of God.
