# **The Semiotic Architecture of Revelation: An Empirical Validation of the Swedenborgian Canon Through Comparative Hermeneutics and Structural Isomorphism**

## **1\. Introduction: The Epistemological Crisis of the Biblical Canon**

The theological corpus of Emanuel Swedenborg (1688–1772) posits one of the most radical restructuring of the biblical canon in the history of Christian thought. Unlike the Marcionite rejection of the Old Testament or the Lutheran critique of the "strawy epistles," Swedenborg’s reclassification of Scripture is not based on doctrinal preference or historical doubts, but on a rigid, purported metaphysical law: the **Science of Correspondences**. This doctrine asserts that the natural world and the spiritual world are linked not by arbitrary metaphor, but by a functional, causal relationship wherein natural objects and events serve as the "ultimates" or containers for specific spiritual realities.  
Within this framework, Swedenborg bifurcates the traditional Protestant canon into two distinct categories:

1. **The Word (*Verbum*):** Texts dictated by "Immediate Inspiration" from the Lord, containing a continuous, unbroken "internal sense" that details the regeneration of the human mind and the glorification of the Divine Human. This includes the Pentateuch, Joshua, Judges, Samuel, Kings, the Psalms, the Prophets, the Gospels, and Revelation.  
2. **The Writings (*Hagiographa* / *Apostolic*):** Texts produced by "Mediate Inspiration," filtered through the intelligence and personality of the human author. These are "good books for the church," possessed of high doctrinal value and illumination, but lacking the infinite, contiguous spiritual series that defines the Divine style. This category includes Chronicles, Ezra, Nehemiah, Esther, Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Song of Solomon, Acts, and the Epistles.

### **1.1 The Charge of Arbitrariness**

This classification invites a profound methodological critique: the charge of "cherry-picking." Critics argue that Swedenborg arbitrarily selected books that facilitated his allegorical exegesis while relegating recalcitrant texts—such as the Pauline Epistles, which emphasize forensic justification over regenerative transformation—to a secondary status. If the "internal sense" is merely a subjective superimposition by the reader, then the distinction between "The Word" and "The Writings" is illusory.  
However, if the "Science of Correspondences" is, as Swedenborg claims, a universal semiotic law (comparable to the relationship between a smile and happiness), then it should be empirically testable. The text of "The Word" should exhibit structural properties—specifically, **translatable correspondential perception**—that are demonstrably absent in "The Writings."

### **1.2 Research Objective**

This report extends the research parameters beyond Swedenborg’s primary exegetical works (*Arcana Coelestia* and *Apocalypse Revealed*) to empirically test the doctrine of correspondences against biblical books *not* previously handled in a continuous exposition. By applying a rigorous hermeneutic stress test to **historical doublets** (parallel narratives in Samuel/Kings vs. Chronicles) and **epistolary motifs** (Apostolic letters), we aim to determine whether the "internal sense" is an objective structural feature or a theological fiction.  
The inquiry is driven by the following hypothesis: *If Swedenborg’s canon is valid, the application of correspondential keys to "Word" books will yield a coherent, sequential spiritual narrative, while the application of the same keys to "Writings" books will result in hermeneutic fragmentation, incoherence, or static doctrinal abstraction.*

## **2\. Theoretical Framework and Methodology**

To validate the distinction without presupposing Swedenborg’s authority, we employ an independent methodological framework characterized by three primary analytical tools.

### **2.1 The Theory of Vertical Causality vs. Horizontal Metaphor**

Standard literary analysis views biblical symbolism as **horizontal metaphor**—a rhetorical device invented by the author to illustrate a point (e.g., "The Lord is my shepherd"). Swedenborgian hermeneutics posits **vertical causality**—the symbol exists in the text because the spiritual reality *caused* the natural event or object to exist in the narrative, often bypassing the conscious intent of the human writer.

* **Immediate Inspiration (The Word):** The writer is an instrument; the specific words, numbers, and names are dictated to form a continuous vessel for the internal sense.  
* **Mediate Inspiration (The Writings):** The writer is illuminated; the text reflects their own intellect, doctrinal understanding, and rhetorical choices.

### **2.2 The Historical Doublet Stress Test**

This methodology leverages the existence of parallel narratives in the Hebrew Bible. The books of **Samuel** and **Kings** (classified as *Word*) often describe the same events as the books of **Chronicles** (classified as *Writings*). However, these accounts frequently diverge in details, numbers, and attribution of agency.

* **Test Protocol:** We apply standard correspondential keys to the divergent details. If the "Word" version yields a coherent spiritual meaning while the "Writings" version yields a broken or disordered meaning, the classification is validated.

### **2.3 The Epistolary Mode Analysis**

This test compares the usage of shared symbols (e.g., "Cloud," "Oil") in the **Gospels/Revelation** (*Word*) versus the **Epistles** (*Writings*).

* **Test Protocol:** We determine if the symbol functions as an **Organic Correspondence** (consistent, ontological link across the canon) or a **Rhetorical Allegory** (variable, illustrative metaphor).

## **3\. The Control Group: Establishing the Baseline of "The Word"**

Before analyzing the exclusions, we must establish that the books classified as "The Word" actually possess the requisite internal structure, particularly in obscure or morally problematic passages that seemingly defy rational exegesis.

### **3.1 The Semiotics of Retribution: Elisha and the Bears (2 Kings 2\)**

The narrative of Elisha in **2 Kings 2:23–24** describes the prophet traveling to Bethel, where he is mocked by "little children" (or youths) who chant "Go up, thou bald head." Elisha curses them, and two she-bears emerge from the woods to tear 42 of them apart. Critics cite this as evidence of a primitive, vindictive morality. However, under correspondential analysis, the text reveals a rigorous, impersonal description of the laws of spiritual cause and effect.

#### **3.1.1 The Correspondence of Baldness**

In the Swedenborgian system, the body corresponds to the spirit. The head corresponds to the highest wisdom, and the hair—being the outermost covering—corresponds to the **literal sense of the Word**. Hair clothes the head just as the literal stories clothe the internal wisdom. To call the prophet (who represents the Word) "bald" is to deny the sanctity and divinity of the literal sense—to claim the Bible is merely a human book, stripped of its divine vesture. The "youths" at Bethel (the House of God/Church) represent those within the church who have knowledge but mock the Word's external holiness.

#### **3.1.2 The Power of the Bear**

The **bear** corresponds to the power of the natural/literal sense of the Word. In nature, the bear is a powerful, plantigrade omnivore, often associated with the obscure caves of the earth. Spiritually, it represents the literal text: obscure to the uninitiated, rough in texture, but possessing immense power to defend the internal truth. When the "youths" mock the "hair" (literal sense), the "bears" (the power of that literal sense) are summoned to destroy the mocking state.

#### **3.1.3 The Arcanum of 42**

The number **42** is not accidental. It appears in Revelation 11:2 and 13:5 as the duration ("forty-two months") of the beast's dominion and the treading down of the holy city. Swedenborg identifies 42 as signifying "blasphemy" or the full duration of a state of vastation (spiritual destruction) until the end.

* **Calculation:** 42 \= 6 (labor/combat) × 7 (holiness). It represents the profanation of the holy through spiritual combat or denial.

**Result:** The narrative is not a story of child abuse but a semiotic equation: *When the external church (Bethel) enters a state of total profanation (42) by denying the divinity of the Word’s letter (baldness), it is destroyed by the very power of that letter (bears).* The text demonstrates **translatable correspondential perception**; the moral difficulty vanishes, replaced by a coherent description of spiritual law.

### **3.2 The Census of David: 2 Samuel 24**

In **2 Samuel 24**, God moves David to number the people, an act that results in a plague destroying 70,000 men.

* **The Sin:** To "number" corresponds to **ordination** and **arrangement** from the self-hood (proprium). It signifies the attempt to quantify one's spiritual assets (truths and goods) as one's own possession, rather than acknowledging them as the Lord's.  
* **The Numbers:** The census yields **800,000** men of Israel and **500,000** men of Judah.  
  * **500:** Corresponds to "fullness" or "much" (5 x 100). Judah corresponds to **Celestial Love** (Will). The number signifies that the Will is in a state of potential fullness.  
  * **800:** Corresponds to **Spiritual Truth** (Intellect). The number 8 signifies a new beginning or the intensity of temptation (the 8th day/circumcision). It indicates the Intellect is prepared for spiritual combat.

**Result:** The numbers in 2 Samuel are derivates of 5 and 8, which carry specific regenerative meanings in the internal sense (Remains and Temptations). The narrative coherently describes the "Spiritual Man" (David) falling into the pride of self-intelligence regarding his spiritual state.

## **4\. Analysis I: The Hagiographic Divergence (Kings vs. Chronicles)**

We now apply the **Historical Doublet Stress Test**. If Swedenborg’s exclusion of Chronicles from "The Word" is valid, the parallel accounts in Chronicles should fail to exhibit the correspondential integrity found in Samuel and Kings. They should show evidence of "human editing" or "doctrinal correction" that disrupts the spiritual series.

### **4.1 The Agency of Incitement: 2 Samuel 24 vs. 1 Chronicles 21**

**The Textual Divergence:**

* **2 Samuel 24:1 (The Word):** "And again the anger of the LORD was kindled against Israel, and he moved David against them to say, Go, number Israel and Judah."  
* **1 Chronicles 21:1 (The Writings):** "And Satan stood up against Israel, and provoked David to number Israel."

**Hermeneutic Analysis:** Modern theologians reconcile this by stating God "permitted" Satan to tempt David. However, from the perspective of the *Internal Sense*, the divergence is fatal to Chronicles' status as "The Word."

1. **The Word (Samuel):** The internal sense deals with **Appearance**. To the unregenerate mind (represented by Israel in this state), the operation of Divine Truth appears as "Anger." The Lord *appears* to incite the trial because the trial is necessary for purification. This paradox is essential for the internal sense, which describes the Lord's operation in the *inner* man. The attribution of the act to the Lord preserves the Monistic integrity of the text—everything flows from the Divine, even if received as wrath by the wicked.  
2. **The Writings (Chronicles):** The Chronicler, writing post-exile, engages in **Doctrinal Correction**. Uncomfortable with the idea of God inciting sin, the human author inserts "Satan" (the Adversary) to clear God's character. While theologically "correct" in a doctrinal sense, this destroys the *correspondence*. "Satan" breaks the container. By shifting agency to a dualistic adversary, the text becomes a historical/moral lesson rather than a psychological mirror of the Divine presence in temptation. It moves from *Ontological Symbol* (Anger) to *Dogmatic Explanation* (Satan).

**Finding:** 1 Chronicles 21 fails the agency test. It operates as a commentary on the event rather than a vessel for the internal sense.

### **4.2 The Semiotics of Numbers: The Census Discrepancy**

**The Textual Divergence:**

* **2 Samuel 24:9 (The Word):** Israel \= 800,000; Judah \= 500,000.  
* **1 Chronicles 21:5 (The Writings):** Israel \= 1,100,000; Judah \= 470,000.

**Correspondential Analysis:**

* **Samuel (800k / 500k):** As established, these numbers map to the **Spiritual** (8/Combat) and **Celestial** (5/Fullness) degrees. They possess a "spiritual harmonic" essential for the series.  
* **Chronicles (1.1M / 470k):**  
  * **1,100,000:** The number 11 in Swedenborgian hermeneutics signifies a state of **disjunction**, incompleteness, or disorder (being 10 \+ 1, exceeding the state of remains but not reaching 12). It represents a state where the truths of faith are not yet conjoined with charity. While 1.1 million might be historically "accurate" (including standing armies), semiotically it introduces "noise" that disrupts the regenerative sequence.  
  * **470,000:** The number 47 is a prime number with no established correspondence in the Writings. It resolves to 47 x 10,000. Unlike 500 (Fullness), 470 suggests a mere historical tally. The drop from the resonant "500" to the precise but spiritually opaque "470" indicates that the author was recording census data (history) rather than receiving a dictation of spiritual states (revelation).

**Finding:** The numbers in Chronicles exhibit **Historical Particularity** rather than **Correspondential Universality**. They represent data, not spiritual states.

### **4.3 The Architectural Deformity: Solomon’s Porch (1 Kings 6 vs. 2 Chronicles 3\)**

The description of Solomon's Temple provides the most visually striking evidence of the distinction between the texts.  
**The Textual Divergence:**

* **1 Kings 6:2–3 (The Word):** The Temple house is **30 cubits** high. The porch (*ulam*) is 20 cubits wide and 10 cubits deep. The height is not specified, but architectural proportion and the silence of the text imply it aligns with the main structure (approx. 30 cubits).  
* **2 Chronicles 3:4 (The Writings):** The porch height is explicitly stated as **120 cubits**.

**Hermeneutic Analysis:**

1. **The Grand Man:** In correspondence, the Temple represents the **Divine Human** and the **Heavenly Form**. The sanctuary is the body; the porch is the face or entrance.  
2. **Architectural Impossibility:** A 120-cubit porch (approx. 180–200 feet) attached to a 30-cubit (45 feet) building creates a grotesque disproportion. It would resemble a massive skyscraper chimney or tower standing before a low hall. Historical critics universally regard this as a scribal error or a hyperbolic exaggeration by the Chronicler to emphasize the grandeur of the post-exilic hope.  
3. **Semiotic Deformity:** If the Temple corresponds to the Human Form, a "Face" (Porch) that is four times the height of the "Body" (Temple) represents a monstrous spiritual condition. It signifies a state where the *external appearance* (height/glory) vastly outstrips the *internal reality* (the actual house).  
4. **The Number 120:** While 120 has significance (remains of faith), its application here creates a **structural deformity** in the correspondence. The Kings account, by omitting the hyperbolic height, preserves the harmonic proportions necessary to represent the "Grand Man."

**Finding:** The Chronicles account fails the **Structural Isomorphism** test. The inclusion of the 120-cubit height transforms the Temple from a correspondential image of the Divine Human into a historical or rhetorical exaggeration, validating its classification as "Writings" (human impression) rather than "Word" (Divine dictation).

## **5\. Analysis II: The Epistolary Divergence (Gospels vs. Epistles)**

We now apply the **Epistolary Mode Analysis**. Swedenborg excludes the Epistles (Paul, James, Peter, Jude, Hebrews) from the Word, classifying them as "Mediate Inspiration"—teachings from the Holy Spirit filtered through the mind of the apostle. If this is true, the symbols used in the Epistles should function as **Literary Metaphors** rather than **Ontological Correspondences**.

### **5.1 The "Cloud" Variable: Hebrews 12:1 vs. The Gospels**

**The Symbol:** Cloud (*nubes* / *nephele*).  
**The Word (Gospels/Revelation):**

* *Matthew 24:30:* "They shall see the Son of Man coming in the **clouds** of heaven with power and great glory."  
* *Revelation 1:7:* "Behold, he cometh with **clouds**."  
* **Swedenborgian Correspondence:** Clouds correspond to the **Literal Sense of the Word**. Just as a physical cloud tempers the heat and light of the sun (making it endurable for the eye), the literal sense tempers the Divine Wisdom (making it accessible to the natural mind). The "coming in the clouds" signifies the revelation of the internal sense *through* the literal text. This correspondence is structurally consistent from Exodus 19 (Sinai) to Revelation.

**The Writings (Epistles):**

* *Hebrews 12:1:* "Seeing we also are compassed about with so great a **cloud of witnesses**...".  
* **Analysis:** In this text, "cloud" (*nephos*) is used as a **simile** for density or a large multitude. It refers to the aggregation of people (the heroes of faith listed in Chapter 11).  
* **Hermeneutic Break:** If we apply the Swedenborgian key (Cloud \= Literal Sense), Hebrews 12:1 would read: *"Compassed about by a great Literal Sense of Witnesses."* This is incoherent. The author of Hebrews is using a literary trope common in Greek rhetoric (using "cloud" to mean "swarm" or "mass"). The usage is **horizontal** (describing a quantity of people) rather than **vertical** (describing a mechanism of divine influx). It fails the **Universality** test.

### **5.2 The "Oil" Variable: James 5:14 vs. The Psalms**

**The Symbol:** Oil (*elaion*).  
**The Word (Psalms/Samuel):**

* *Psalm 23:5:* "Thou anointest my head with **oil**."  
* *1 Samuel 16:13:* Samuel anoints David with oil, and the Spirit of the Lord comes upon him.  
* **Swedenborgian Correspondence:** Oil corresponds to the **Good of Love** (Celestial Good). Anointing signifies the inauguration of the Divine Good into the humanity or the mind. It is an ontological substance; the oil *is* the love.

**The Writings (Epistles):**

* *James 5:14:* "Is any sick among you?... anointing him with **oil** in the name of the Lord".  
* **Analysis:** James prescribes oil as a **ritual** or **medicinal** act ("aleipho") alongside prayer. Whether viewed as first-century medicine or a sacramental token, the oil is an *instrument* or *symbol* of the healing, not the healing good itself.  
* **Hermeneutic Break:** In the Word, the mention of oil describes a state of being (Love). In James, the mention of oil describes a liturgical practice. Swedenborg notes that in the Apostolic church, these rituals were permitted as external representatives, but the *text* of James describes the *practice*, whereas the *text* of the Word *is* the oil itself. The shift is from **Substance** (Word) to **Ceremony** (Writings). The correspondence does not hold as an internal series; applying "Celestial Good" directly to James 5:14 ("rubbing him with Celestial Good") abstracts the text away from its clear instructional intent regarding church order.

### **5.3 The Pauline Abstraction: Romans vs. Revelation**

**The Word:** Operates via **concrete imagery**. "The Dragon stood before the Woman" (Rev 12). There is no abstract theology here; the theology must be derived from the symbols. **The Writings (Paul):** Operates via **abstract argumentation**. "Therefore we conclude that a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law" (Romans 3:28).  
**The Test:** One cannot apply correspondences to abstract concepts. There is no correspondence for "Justification" or "Imputation" because these are already intellectual abstractions. Correspondences bridge *Nature* and *Spirit*. Paul writes almost entirely within the realm of *Intellect/Doctrine*. Therefore, Paul's letters cannot contain an internal sense because they lack the "natural vessel" (the concrete symbol) required to contain it. They are "naked" truths, whereas the Word is "clothed" truth.

## **6\. Analysis III: The Wisdom Divergence (Psalms vs. Proverbs/Esther)**

### **6.1 The Absence of Combat: Proverbs vs. Psalms**

Swedenborg includes the Psalms in the Word but excludes Proverbs, despite both being "Wisdom Literature".

* **The Psalms:** Written in the first person ("I cry unto thee," "My God, why hast thou forsaken me?"). Under correspondence, this maps perfectly to the **Lord’s Glorification**—the temptation combats of Jesus. The "enemies" in Psalms correspond to the Hells; the "I" is the Divine Human undergoing despair and victory.  
* **Proverbs:** Written as didactic instruction ("My son, hear the instruction of thy father").  
* **The Test:** Applying the "Lord's Temptation" key to Proverbs fails. Proverbs 10:1 ("A wise son maketh a glad father") is a moral maxim. If interpreted as the Lord, it becomes a static theological statement, not a *process* of combat. Proverbs represents **Solomonic Wisdom** (human reason enlightened by God), whereas Psalms represents **Davidic Combat** (Divine Truth fighting evil). The difference is between *statutes of life* (Proverbs/Writings) and the *source of Life* (Psalms/Word).

### **6.2 The Secular Void: The Book of Esther**

The Book of Esther is unique in the canon for arguably originating from a secular or political motive, and notably, the name of God does not appear in the Hebrew text.

* **Swedenborgian View:** It lacks the internal sense entirely.  
* **Empirical Test:** Attempting to correspond "Ahasuerus" or "Haman" yields no consistent spiritual series found elsewhere in the Word. In the Word, "King" corresponds to Divine Truth. Ahasuerus, however, acts capriciously, divorcing Vashti for disobedience and sanctioned genocide. While Christian allegorists have tried to force Christological meaning onto Esther (Esther \= Christ interceding), the correspondential key fails because the **political causality** (court intrigue) is too dense. The text operates on the plane of *Civil History*, not *Vertical Causality*. Its exclusion validates the test: Swedenborg rejected a popular, patriotic book because it failed the semiotic pressure test of the internal sense.

## **7\. Comparative Synthesis: The Jachin and Boaz of the Canon**

The two pillars of Solomon's Temple, **Jachin** ("He shall establish") and **Boaz** ("In him is strength"), offer a final meta-symbol for this investigation.

* **The Word** corresponds to **Jachin**: It establishes the Divine Truth in the heavens.  
* **The Writings** correspond to **Boaz**: They provide the strength of doctrine for the church on earth.

## **8\. Conclusion: Refuting the Cherry-Picking Charge**

The investigation demonstrates that Swedenborg’s canon is not a product of arbitrary "cherry-picking" but the result of a rigorous application of semiotic laws.  
**Findings:**

1. **Translatable Perception in "The Word":** Books classified as "The Word" demonstrate a latent, coherent structure where historical details—even the "weird" ones like 42 boys and bears—translate into a consistent theology of regeneration when the correspondence key is applied. The obscurity of the literal text is a feature, not a bug; it forces the mind to the internal sense.  
2. **Hermeneutic Failure in "The Writings":** When the same keys are applied to Chronicles or the Epistles, the system generates "noise." The 120-cubit porch creates a monstrous spiritual form; the introduction of Satan in Chronicles disrupts the monistic flow of Divine Providence; the "Cloud" in Hebrews dissolves into a crowd rather than the Word.  
3. **The Definition of Inspiration:** The distinction is not between "True" and "False" or "Divine" and "Human," but between **Immediate Inspiration** (Dictation/The Word) and **Mediate Inspiration** (Illumination/The Writings).

The "Writings" (Epistles, Chronicles) are confirmed as essential *doctrinal* supports for the Church—they explain the faith clearly to the rational mind. However, they are structurally incapable of bearing the infinite internal sense. Swedenborg’s exclusion of them is thus an act of **semiotic precision**, preserving the specific function of the "Word" as a conduit of conjunction with the Divine Human, distinct from the "Writings" which serve to instruct the church. The evidence suggests that the "Internal Sense" is an objective property of the specific texts Swedenborg identified, a property that cannot be forced onto the rest of the canon without breaking the code.
