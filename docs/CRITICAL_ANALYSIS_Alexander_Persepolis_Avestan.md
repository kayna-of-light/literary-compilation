## 2. SCHOLARLY CONSENSUS VS. MINORITY VIEWS

### 2.1 Claims Representing Mainstream Consensus ✅

1. **The Avesta was primarily oral in the Achaemenid period**
   - STATUS: MAINSTREAM CONSENSUS
   - Confirmed by Kellens (Encyclopaedia Iranica), Hoffmann, and majority of Iranologists

2. **Alexander burned Persepolis in 330 BCE**
   - STATUS: UNDISPUTED HISTORICAL FACT

3. **The Persepolis Fortification Archive was preserved by the fire**
   - STATUS: MAINSTREAM CONSENSUS
   - Confirmed by Oriental Institute publications

4. **Bullae prove the existence of parchment documents that were destroyed**
   - STATUS: MAINSTREAM CONSENSUS
   - Encyclopaedia Iranica confirms this interpretation

5. **No written Avesta existed in the form claimed by Sasanian sources**
   - STATUS: MAINSTREAM CONSENSUS

6. **The "Burned Book" narrative served Sasanian ideological purposes**
   - STATUS: MAINSTREAM CONSENSUS
   - Supported by Kotwal & Kreyenbroek, de Jong

### 2.2 Claims Representing Minority or Contested Views ⚠️

1. **The Hermippus "two million verses" as Pseudepigrapha**
   - STATUS: MAJORITY VIEW but with some dissenters
   - Some scholars have argued these reflect genuine Iranian material

2. **The Arsacid Avesta hypothesis (Andreas)**
   - STATUS: LARGELY REJECTED
   - Document correctly notes this was refuted by Bailey, Henning, Morgenstierne (1940s)
   - Correctly characterized as abandoned

3. **Dating of Old Avestan texts to ~1000 BCE**
   - STATUS: CONTESTED
   - Document presents as "general consensus" but dating remains highly disputed
   - Some scholars argue for significantly earlier dates (1500-1200 BCE)

4. **Islamic conquest as "the Real Destruction"**
   - STATUS: INTERPRETIVE (not strictly minority)
   - This is a valid scholarly position but framed somewhat polemically in the document

### 2.3 Potentially Overstated Claims ⚠️

1. *"The scholarly consensus maintains that the Avesta was an oral corpus in the Achaemenid period. Therefore, Alexander could not have burned a 'Zoroastrian Bible' because no such physical object existed."*
   
   **Issue:** While this is the majority view, it's stated too absolutely. Some scholars (including aspects of Boyce's position) allow for the possibility of limited written materials. The binary framing oversimplifies.

2. *"It is virtually certain that the Magi at Persepolis recited these texts exactly as they are preserved today."*
   
   **Issue:** Overstated. While the Gathas show remarkable preservation, claiming "exactly as preserved today" ignores known transmission variants and phonetic changes.
