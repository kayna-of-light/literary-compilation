### **1.1 The Epistemological Framework: Swedenborg’s Semiotics**

To assess the validity of the comparison between Disney’s cinematic language and Swedenborg’s theology, one must first define the precise mechanism of Swedenborgian correspondence. Unlike simple allegory, which Swedenborg dismisses as arbitrary invention or poetic simile, correspondence is defined as a causal, ontological relationship. It is not that a lion reminds the viewer of courage in a metaphorical sense; it is that the spiritual affection of the "power of truth" causes the form of a lion to exist in the natural world. The natural form is the "ultimate" container for the spiritual cause.  
Swedenborg’s system rests on three pillars that are particularly relevant to animation analysis:

* **Vertical Causality (Influx):** The spiritual world is the realm of causes; the natural world is the realm of effects. Life flows from the Divine, through the spiritual degrees, into natural forms. The quality of the form determines the reception of the influx. A distorted form (a villain or a wasteland) receives the same life force but twists it into evil or decay.  
* **Discrete Degrees:** Reality is stratified into distinct levels that do not merge but communicate via correspondence. The "Celestial" degree (pertaining to Love and the Will) flows into the "Spiritual" degree (pertaining to Wisdom and the Intellect), which in turn flows into the "Natural" degree (pertaining to Action and Form).  
* **The Grand Man (Maximus Homo):** Swedenborg posits that the entire heaven is structured in the form of a single human being, and by extension, human society and the church function as a "collective man." The health of this collective body depends on the proper subordination of the "natural" (feet/extremities) to the "spiritual" and "celestial" (heart/head).

In the context of animation, the animator plays the role of the "spiritual cause," infusing *anima* (life/soul) into the "natural" forms on screen. Thus, the physics of the Disney universe are inherently spiritual physics: gravity, light, color, and biology obey the emotional and moral logic of the scene rather than the laws of Newtonian mechanics. This artistic necessity aligns the medium perfectly with Swedenborg’s vision of a plastic, responsive spiritual world where "affections... manifest visibly as animals" and landscapes shift instantaneously to reflect the interior state of the inhabitants.

### **1.2 The Distinction: Correspondence vs. Metaphor**

A critical methodological distinction must be drawn between *Correspondence* and *Metaphor* (or Allegory) to understand the user's critique of the modern "Cultural Trust" approach.

* **Metaphor/Allegory (Arbitrary/Mechanical):** These are human-invented rhetorical devices. In an allegory, the relationship is a mechanical substitution (e.g., a pair of scales representing justice) that requires a codebook to decipher. It is "horizontal"—relating one natural object to an abstract concept based on human agreement.  
* **Correspondence (Organic/Symbolic):** This is a universal law where the natural world is a causal mirror of the spiritual world. A correspondence is "discovered" rather than "invented" and is inherent in the object's nature. It is "vertical"—relating a natural effect to its spiritual cause.

The user argues that modern Disney films, by relying on external cultural advisors, treat symbols as cultural metaphors (horizontal) rather than spiritual correspondences (vertical). They adopt the *form* (e.g., Maui's hair) because a specific culture believes it has power, rather than presenting it as a universal law of spiritual influx that necessitates hair as the "ultimate" of the natural.

## **II. The Architecture of the Invisible: Historical Roots of the Disney Ethos**

To understand why the Disney Studio would actively engage with a specific 18th-century theological system, one must first understand the artistic crisis that threatened the studio in the early 1930s. This was not a financial crisis, but a metaphysical one: the "Crisis of Ensoulment".

### **2.1 The Crisis of the Rubber Hose**

In the 1920s, American animation was dominated by the "rubber hose" style. Characters like Felix the Cat or the early Mickey Mouse were constructed of loose, flexible tubes. They moved with a fluid, rhythmic bounce that was entertaining but fundamentally mechanical. They had no weight, no bone structure, and most importantly, no interiority. If a character moved, it was because the animator moved the pencil, not because the character had a thought or a feeling that caused the movement.  
Walt Disney realized by the early 1930s that this style had a distinct ceiling. He envisioned feature-length films that could sustain an audience's emotional investment for eighty minutes. To achieve this, his characters needed to appear to have a soul. They needed to seem self-motivated. This required a paradigm shift from "animating movement" to "animating intentions". This shift necessitated a new physics—one where the internal will was the primary mover of the external form.

### **2.2 The Swedenborgian Solution: The Doctrine of Influx**

Emanuel Swedenborg’s theology offered a precise mechanism for this problem. Swedenborg taught the doctrine of Influx, which structures reality into discrete degrees:

* **The Spiritual (Cause):** The realm of Will, Affection, and Thought.  
* **The Natural (Effect):** The realm of Body, Action, and Form.

The mechanism is explicit: Life flows from the Spiritual into the Natural. The body does not move itself; the spirit moves the body. Therefore, the shape of the body and its actions are "correspondences" of the spirit within. Swedenborg wrote in *Arcana Coelestia*: "Action is nothing but the will flowing forth... The body is the obedience".  
For an animator facing the Crisis of Ensoulment, this is the Holy Grail. To make a drawing look alive, one must not draw the body moving; one must draw the affection (the spiritual cause) that results in the body moving. This alignment of artistic necessity with theological doctrine created a vacuum at the Disney Studio that pulled in Swedenborgian thinkers and methods. The studio began to actively seek out anyone who could teach this principle of "Inner Force driving Outer Form".

### **2.3 Don Graham and the Pedagogy of Forces**

The primary vector for this "active substrate" was Don Graham, the art instructor hired by Walt Disney in 1932 to retrain the studio's artists. Graham’s pedagogy, known as "Action Analysis," was a secularized reinstatement of Swedenborg’s doctrine of Influx. Before Graham, animation training focused on "Form"—how to draw a muscle, how to draw a joint. This led to the "rubber hose" stiffness. Graham introduced a radical new methodology: he taught that the animator must not draw the thing, but the force driving the thing.  
In his lecture notes from 1937, Graham speaks of the "inner force" that dictates the "outer form." A famous memo from Walt Disney to Don Graham in 1935 explicitly articulates this philosophy: "The driving force behind the action is the mood, the personality, the attitude of the character – or all three. Therefore the mind is the pilot". This pedagogy posits that the physical world (the drawing) is merely the "obedience" of the spiritual world (the character's intent). The animator does not manipulate the body; the animator manipulates the will, and the body follows.  
Research links Graham and the Chouinard Art Institute (where he taught) to the broader currents of "Theosophy" and "Metaphysics" prevalent in California art circles at the time. Theosophy, heavily indebted to Swedenborg (via Blavatsky and earlier mystics), preached that thoughts were "things" with shape and color. Graham’s classes, often described as "metaphysical" by students like Walt Stanchfield, trained Disney animators to see the material world as a shell for spiritual energies. This training primed the entire studio culture to accept stories where internal moral states dictated external physical reality. When an animator drew a storm cloud in *Fantasia* or *Bambi*, they weren't drawing water vapor; they were drawing "anger" or "menace." This is the definition of Correspondence. By institutionalizing this training, Disney built a studio that thought like Swedenborgians, even if they didn't pray like them.

### **2.4 The Appleseed Nexus: A Case Study in Intentional Coding**

The argument that this influence was purely accidental is dismantled by the production history of *The Legend of Johnny Appleseed* (1948). This film serves as the "smoking gun" for intentional theological coding within the studio.  
Johnny Appleseed (John Chapman) was a real historical figure and a missionary for the Church of the New Jerusalem (Swedenborgianism). When Disney decided to adapt his life, they were faced with a dilemma: how to portray a man whose entire life was driven by a specific, sectarian theology without alienating a mass audience. Rather than erasing Chapman's faith, the studio engaged in a process of "active translation".  
Archival correspondence reveals that the studio hired Carl Carmer, a folklore consultant and expert in the "Burned-Over District" (the hotbed of 19th-century American religious movements), to navigate this history. The studio also corresponded with Florence Murdoch, a Swedenborgian archivist. Through these channels, the studio ensured that the film's "visual theology" remained faithful to Swedenborgian doctrine, even if the explicit sectarian labels were removed.  
The film encodes the "Blessings Three"—Love, Faith, and the Apple Tree—into its central song. This is a direct translation of the Swedenborgian Trinity of Essentials: Love (the Will), Faith (the Understanding), and Use (Works). Furthermore, the film's depiction of Johnny's "Guardian Angel" adheres to Swedenborg’s specific angelology, which teaches that all angels were once humans who lived on earth. The angel in the film is not a distinct species with wings, but a "pioneer spirit"—a deceased human dressed in frontier garb. This specific detail proves that the studio was not merely copying generic Christian tropes but was actively engaging with Swedenborgian distinctions.

## **III. The Universal Grammar: The Science of Correspondences in the Golden Age**

Having established the historical and pedagogical "substrate," we turn to the "Golden Age" and "Renaissance" films (roughly 1937–1999) to analyze how this "Science of Correspondences" manifested as a universal symbolic logic. In these films, the correspondence between the spiritual and the natural is treated as an objective, universal law, consistent with Swedenborg’s claim that correspondences are "discovered" rather than "invented".

### **3.1 The King and the Land: Topography as Moral Mirror**

One of the most persistent and structurally significant tropes in the Disney canon is the intrinsic, causal link between the moral condition of the monarch and the physical health of the environment. This narrative device, known in comparative mythology and literary criticism as the "Fisher King" archetype, is structurally identical to Swedenborg’s correspondence of the "Land" or "Earth" with the "State of the Church" (the collective spiritual state of humanity).  
In Swedenborg’s exegesis, the physical state of the land in biblical prophecy—whether it is blooming, drought-stricken, or frozen—is never a description of agriculture but a precise diagnosis of the spiritual state of the people. Disney films replicate this diagnostic topography with high fidelity.  
**The Lion King (1994)** serves as a perfect model of Swedenborg’s *Maximus Homo* (The Grand Man). The Kingdom is a body. Mufasa is the "Head" (Wisdom/Government). The Lionesses are the "Heart/Lungs" (Vital operations). The Hyenas are the "Intestines/Excretory function" (consuming waste). When Scar (the Proprium/Self-Love) usurps the Head, the entire Body (Land) sickens. The resulting drought is not meteorological; it is "spiritual vastation." The water (Truth) dries up because the connection to the spiritual source (Mufasa/The Sun) has been severed. The return of the true King (Truth) brings immediate rain (Influx of Truth), which restores the land instantaneously.  
**Frozen (2013)** presents a similar correspondence. Arendelle’s eternal winter is a literalization of the metaphor "frozen heart." Swedenborg describes "frigid zones" in the spiritual world inhabited by those who have isolated themselves from the warmth of Charity (Love). Elsa’s fear creates a world of ice (Truth without Good). Ice, in Swedenborgian symbology, corresponds to truth that has become hard, brittle, and unyielding because it lacks the heat of love. The "thaw" can only be achieved by an act of true love (Charity), which restores the liquid state (Truth flowing with Good).  
In these narratives, the "Golden Age" is not a time period but a spiritual state—a state of "Divine Order" where the natural world perfectly reflects the spiritual governance of a righteous King. The landscape is a moral mirror, providing a "universal" diagnosis of the spiritual health of the characters.

### **3.2 Animal Correspondences: The Menagerie of the Mind**

Swedenborg asserted that animals correspond to specific human "affections." Disney films adhere to this taxonomy with remarkable consistency, utilizing animals not as biological entities but as externalized aspects of the human psyche.  
**The Horse (Intellect):** A central Swedenborgian correspondence is Horse \= Understanding/Intellect. In the Ancient Near East and Greek mythology (e.g., Pegasus), the horse represents power, swiftness, and guidance. Disney films consistently use the horse to represent the "Intellectual Principle."

* In *Tangled*, Maximus is the "Intellect" obsessed with the "Law." He is rigid, judgmental, and relentless until guided by Rapunzel (Affection).  
* In *Frozen II*, the Nokk (Water Horse) represents the "Natural Understanding." Elsa (the Spiritual Mind) must tame and ride the horse, symbolizing the spiritual mind's control over the natural intellect.  
* In *Sleeping Beauty*, Samson represents the "Power of Truth" that carries the Prince into battle against Falsity (Maleficent).

**The Bird (Rational Thought):** Because they fly above the earth, birds correspond to intellectual ideas and rational concepts.

* **Zazu (*The Lion King*)** represents "administrative truth" or orderly thinking. He is the bureaucrat of the mind, concerned with rules and boundaries.  
* **Scuttle (*The Little Mermaid*)** represents "false rationality"—the tendency of the sensory mind to make incorrect deductions (e.g., a fork is a "dinglehopper").

**The Dog (Natural Affection):** Dogs correspond to "natural good"—loyalty, friendship, and the simple affections of the natural mind. Characters like Bruno (*Cinderella*) or Max (*The Little Mermaid*) represent the intuitive ability of the natural mind to perceive good and evil more clearly than the rational mind (which can be deceived).  
This system of animal correspondences functions as a "Universal Grammar." It assumes that the meaning of a "Horse" or a "Lion" is inherent in the animal's nature (its function), not culturally assigned. A Lion represents the "Power of Truth" because of its strength and dominance, regardless of whether the story is set in Africa or England. This aligns with the Swedenborgian view that correspondences are "organic" and "discovered," not "invented".

### **3.3 Weather and Elements: The Pathetic Fallacy as Spiritual Science**

Weather events in Disney films are rarely meteorological; they are "spiritual physics" that respond to emotional and moral shifts. This utilization of the "Pathetic Fallacy" (attributing human emotion to nature) is, in the Disney context, a rigorous application of the Science of Correspondences.  
**Rain/Water:** Corresponds to "Truth" and "Cleansing." The rain at the end of *The Lion King* is the "Influx of Truth" that washes away the falsities of Scar’s reign. In Swedenborgian thought, water corresponds to truth because it cleanses, reflects, and sustains life. **Ice vs. Snow:** Ice represents truth that lacks "Good" (love), becoming hard and brittle. Snow represents natural truth that is cold but can be melted. The interplay between ice and water in *Frozen* is a precise theological drama about the relationship between "Faith Alone" (Ice) and "Charity" (Warmth).  
The "Universal" nature of these symbols in the Golden Age and Renaissance films suggests a belief in a shared, objective spiritual reality. The symbols were treated as "Archetypes"—structures of the psyche that transcend culture. A King was a King, whether in *Aladdin* or *The Little Mermaid*, representing the same principle of Governance/Truth.

The modern era of Disney animation (2010–Present) marks a distinct shift in how this "active substrate" is managed. While the underlying mechanism of "Influx" (inner force driving outer form) remains the technical foundation of the animation, the hermeneutic framework has moved from "Universal Archetypes" to "Cultural Specificity." This shift is institutionalized through the use of "Cultural Trusts"—advisory bodies composed of anthropologists, linguists, and cultural practitioners.  
This section investigates the user's contention that this shift represents a "misunderstanding" where spiritual causality is traded for cultural metaphor, resulting in symbols that mimic correspondence but function as cultural appropriation.

### **4.1 Moana and the Oceanic Story Trust: Mana as Influx**

For the production of *Moana* (2016), Disney established the Oceanic Story Trust. This body was instrumental in shifting the film's depiction of the demigod Maui. Originally designed as bald, the Trust advised that Maui must have hair because "mana" (spiritual power) is held in the hair.  
On the surface, this is a pure application of the Science of Correspondences: a physical attribute (hair) corresponds to a spiritual reality (mana). It aligns with the Swedenborgian concept of the "Nazarite" (Samson), where hair corresponds to the "ultimate of the natural" where spiritual power resides.  
However, the *source* of this correspondence is different. In the Golden Age, the correspondence would have been derived from a "Universal" understanding of strength/virility (the Lion's mane, Samson's hair). In *Moana*, it is derived from specific Polynesian theology. The "Truth" of the symbol is validated not by its universal function, but by its cultural authority.  
The user argues that this reliance on "Cultural Trusts" leads to a "big misunderstanding." The argument suggests that while the *form* (hair \= power) is preserved, the *essence* is treated as a cultural artifact rather than a universal spiritual law. The studio treats "mana" as a specific Polynesian concept to be respected, rather than as a universal principle of Influx that operates in all humans. This creates a "horizontal" validation (validated by the Trust/Culture) rather than a "vertical" validation (validated by the spiritual cause). The audience is led to believe that "Maui has hair because he is Polynesian," not "Maui has hair because spiritual power manifests in the ultimates of the body."

### **4.2 Frozen II and the Verddet: The River of Memory**

*Frozen II* (2019) represents the most explicitly Swedenborgian film in the modern canon, centering on the concept that "Water has memory." The filmmakers collaborated with the Verddet, a Sami advisory group, to ensure the spiritual depiction of the Northuldra people was accurate.  
The film introduces **Ahtohallan**, the "Glacier of Truth," described as the "river of memory." In Swedenborgian thought, water corresponds to Truth/Memory. The journey to Ahtohallan is a journey inward to the "internal memory" where the truth of one's life is stored.  
Here, the tension between universal and cultural becomes acute. The film explicitly frames this spirituality as "indigenous wisdom" belonging to the Northuldra, contrasting it with the "civilized/dam-building" mindset of the Arendellians. In the Golden Age, the "King" (Arendelle) would have represented Divine Truth. In *Frozen II*, the King (Elsa's grandfather) represents "Falsehood/Colonialism." The "Truth" is found in the indigenous culture.  
While the correspondence holds (Water \= Memory), the narrative framing shifts the locus of spiritual authority from the "Universal Church" (the King) to the "Specific Church" (the Northuldra). This aligns with the user's critique: the spiritual logic is filtered through a cultural lens. The audience is taught that "Water has memory" because it is a *Northuldra belief*, not because it is a *universal metaphysical fact*. The universality of the correspondence is tethered to a specific cultural form.

### **4.3 Encanto and the Colombian Trust: Living Correspondence**

In *Encanto* (2021), the "Colombian Cultural Trust" guided the film's use of Magical Realism. The house, **Casita**, is alive. It responds instantly to the emotional state of the family. When the family's relationships (spiritual bonds) fracture, the house (physical container) cracks.

There is no "metaphor" here; the causality is literal within the diegesis. However, the film attributes this mechanics to the specific genre of "Magical Realism" (a Latin American tradition) rather than a universal law of spirit. The "Miracle Candle" represents Divine Influx, providing energy for the "gifts" (Uses). When the family loses sight of the source of the gifts (Love/Charity) and focuses only on the performance, the light fades.  
Again, the Swedenborgian structure is intact: Use (Gifts) must be grounded in Love (Candle/Abuela's intent). But the framing is culturally specific. The "magic" is tied to the specific trauma and history of Colombia. This "active seeking" of the specific cultural form (Magical Realism) is where the user sees a drift.
